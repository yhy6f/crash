Traffic crashes in Columbia

ByJasmine Ye Han May. 4, 2016

Traffic crashes investigated by law enforcement officers have uniform reports in the Statewide Traffic Accident Records System (STARS) by the state highway patrol records division. A look into STARS reports data reveals some interesting patterns.

Chart1: Since 2005, the number of STARS reported crashes in Columbia has dropped by over 60%. The number of 911 calls regarding traffic collisions has remained largely the same. Columbia Police Department changed its accident policy in November 2009 and stopped attending to minor collisions. The increasing gap could mean more crashes going unreported.

Chart2: Despite the policy change, the police are still required to investigate crashes causing injuries and fatalities. In 2015, the number of people injured dropped by 27% compared to 2005.

Chart3: The number of fatalities caused by traffic accidents has been fluctuating from 2005 to 2015, with a trend of increasing after a sharp decrease in 2010.

Source: Missouri State Highway Patrol, City of Columbia Joint Communication.
